A diverse team of scientists and innovators dedicated to advancing biotechnology and nanomedicine.

Frederico Pittella Silva

Based at the Federal University of Juiz de Fora (UFJF), focuses on the development of RNA interference (RNAi) therapies for breast cancer using advanced nanoparticle delivery systems. Led by Dr. Frederico Pittella Silva, the team includes postdoctoral and doctoral researchers who are pioneering novel approaches for targeted gene silencing. Their projects combine nanotechnology, molecular biology, and therapeutic design, emphasizing translational applications and high-impact innovations in RNA-based therapeutics.

Silvia Ligorio Fialho

Operating at Funed, specializes in ocular drug delivery and preclinical evaluation of intravitreal therapies. Under the leadership of Silvia Ligorio Fialho, the group develops protein-loaded liposomes and intravitreal devices coated with nanofibers, aiming to treat retinal vein occlusion and age-related macular degeneration. Their work integrates nanomaterials, biomedical engineering, and in vivo safety studies, supporting the creation of a research core for biopharmaceutical development.

Mauro Aparecido de Sousa Xavier

Based at UNIMONTES, focuses on vaccine development, particularly recombinant multi-epitope vaccines against Rhipicephalus microplus. Luciana Maria Silva Lopes

Operating at Funed, is dedicated to cancer therapeutics using gene editing technologies. Led by Luciana Maria Silva Lopes, the team explores STAT3 gene inactivation via CRISPR/Cas9 in 3D tumor spheroids enriched with cancer stem cells (CD44+/CD24-/low). Their research integrates molecular biology, stem cell technologies, and translational oncology, aiming to discover new therapeutic approaches for aggressive cancers and advancing preclinical modeling tools.

Armando da Silva Cunha Junior

Based at UFMG, focuses on innovative gene therapy and nanomedicine approaches for ocular disorders, including hereditary retinal dystrophies and age-related macular degeneration. Led by Armando da Silva Cunha Junior, the group develops non-viral gene transfer vectors, polymeric nanofibers loaded with bevacizumab, and alternative drug delivery systems. Their work combines biomaterials, pharmacology, and therapeutic engineering to create cutting-edge ocular therapeutics.

Matheus Souza Gomes

Operating at the Federal University of Uberlândia (UFU), PADM2 is centered on bioinformatics and RNA-based technologies. Led by Dr. Matheus S. Gomes, the team develops tools for RNA selection of siRNA (siRNA Seeker) and miRNA, in a web-based aproach, integrating computational biology, data science, and molecular genetics. In addition, the group carries out a 3D protein modeling project powered by the MOE software, enhancing structural analysis and drug design capabilities. The focus is on enabling precision RNA therapeutics and advancing digital tools for gene regulation research.
